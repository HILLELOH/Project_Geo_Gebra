A Polygon is a closed shape formed by three or more straight line segments.

Representation:  vertices V1(x1,y1), V2(x2,y2), …, Vn(xn,yn) connected in order.

Area (Shoelace formula):
  A = ½ · |Σ(xᵢ · yᵢ₊₁ − xᵢ₊₁ · yᵢ)|

Perimeter:
  Sum of all side lengths using the distance formula for each edge.

Types:
  • Convex   — all interior angles < 180°
  • Concave  — at least one interior angle > 180°

Algorithms available:
  Convex Hull — finds the smallest convex polygon enclosing all vertices
                using the Graham scan algorithm (O(n log n)).

  Triangulation — divides the polygon into triangles using Delaunay
                  triangulation, useful for area calculations and rendering.

Interaction:
  Click to add vertices one by one.
  Click the first vertex again to close and complete the polygon.
